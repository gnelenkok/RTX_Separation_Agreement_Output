IV. GENERAL RELEASE OF CLAIMS AND COVENANT NOT TO SUE

If you choose not to sign this Agreement, or if you sign and then revoke it, you will receive only those payments and benefits described in this Section III.


A. GENERAL RELEASE OF CLAIMS: In exchange for the payments and benefits described above in Section II, you agree to release the Company and all of its parents, subsidiaries, divisions, affiliates and successors (collectively, the “Releasees”) from all claims, demands, actions or damages you may have against the Company of whatever kind, including but not limited to those that relate in any way to your employment with the Company or the termination of that employment. You also agree to release from liability each Releasee’s agents, directors, officers, employees, fiduciaries, representatives, successors and assigns (collectively “those associated with the Releasees”). This General Release of Claims covers all claims or demands based on any facts or events, whether known or unknown by you. You expressly release claims arising under the federal Employee Retirement Income Security Act (“ERISA”), the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (“WARN”) Act, the Age Discrimination in Employment Act (“ADEA”), Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, the Equal Pay Act, the Americans with Disabilities Act, the Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act, and the Sarbanes-Oxley Act, all as amended, plus any other federal, state or local law dealing with labor and employment rights or discrimination on any basis, including but not limited to race, color, national origin, sex, genetic information, age, disability, religion, marital status, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, or military or veteran status. You also agree that this General Release of Claims includes claims based on contract (express or implied) or tort theories, whether under common law or otherwise, as well as equitable claims and claims for breach of fiduciary duty. This Agreement also releases claims that you suffered any other harm by or through actions of the Company or those associated with the Company, including, but not limited to claims for defamation or emotional distress, and claims for attorneys’ fees or any other litigation costs.

B. RELEASE EXCLUSIONS AND ADDITIONAL EMPLOYEE PROTECTIONS: Nothing in the General Release of Claims applies to: (i) your vested rights, if any, to pension, savings plan, health or similar benefits under the terms of applicable Company benefits plans or standard policies; (ii) claims arising under any workers’ compensation statute, except claims for wrongful discharge or other discrimination in employment for exercising rights under such a statute; and (iii) claims you may have that arise after the date you sign this Agreement.
Nothing in the General Release of Claims nor anything else in this Agreement limits your rights to: file a charge or complaint with an administrative agency, including the U.S. Equal Employment Opportunity Commission and similar state agencies; provide information to any federal or state agency or participate in an agency investigation or proceeding; or receive money from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (“SEC”) as a reward for providing information to the SEC. You are, however, waiving all rights to recover money (including attorneys’ fees) or other individual relief from any Releasee with respect to any claims that are released in this Agreement.

C. NO FUTURE LAWSUITS / COVENANT NOT TO SUE: In exchange for payments and benefits described in Section II above, besides releasing all claims covered by the General Release above and not excluded under the Release Exclusions above, you promise never to file a lawsuit against the Company, another Releasee or those associated with the Releasees asserting any claim or demand covered by the General Release above. In addition, you agree not to (i) assist any other person or entity in bringing any lawsuit against the Company in any court unless you are compelled to do so by the court or otherwise by law, or (ii) participate in any class or collective action lawsuit against any Releasee or those associated with a Releasee. Excluded from this Covenant Not to Sue are claims to enforce this Agreement or to challenge its validity under the ADEA.
If you sue any Releasee or those associated with a Releasee in violation of this Agreement, you will be required to pay all legal expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, incurred in defending against your suit. Alternatively, if you sue any Releasee or anyone associated with a Releasee in violation of this Agreement, you may, at the defendant’s option, be required to return all Section II monies and benefits paid to you, except for $500.00.

If you sign and do not revoke this Agreement, you promise not to seek hire or rehire for any job with any Releasee at any time in the future, except as a “Releasee” may permit in writing. You also agree that this Agreement is sufficient reason for any Releasee to reject any application you might make for future employment. Finally, you promise not to apply for or otherwise seek to participate in any early retirement or voluntary employee separation plan, program or offer that might be offered by the Company after your termination date.

VI. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND PRIVATE BUSINESS INFORMATION

Whether or not you sign this Agreement, the Intellectual Property Agreement entered into between you and the Company remains in full force and effect after your termination date. The Intellectual Property Agreement states that you will not disclose, use or publish any proprietary, technical or business information developed by, for, or at the expense of the Company, or assigned or entrusted to the Company, unless such information becomes generally known outside the Company (except through breach by you of the Intellectual Property Agreement). Also, you agree not to disclose, use or publish to any person or entity, or assist in the disclosure, publication of or use by any person or entity, any business information that became known to you during your employment with the Company. Business information includes confidential information, privileged legal information, technical, scientific, business or financial information. You must deliver to or leave with the Company all written and other materials containing such information on or before your termination date.
If you sign and do not revoke this Agreement, you agree that: (i) any unauthorized disclosure, violation or threatened violation of the Company’s intellectual property or business information would cause irreparable damage to the trade secret or proprietary status of the Company’s information and to the Company; (ii) the Company will be entitled to an injunction prohibiting you from making any such disclosure, attempted disclosure, violation or threatened violation; and (iii) if you violate the Intellectual Property Agreement, you will be required to pay all legal expenses, including reasonable attorneys’ fees, incurred by the Company in enforcing that agreement.
You shall not be held liable, criminally or civilly, under any Federal or State trade secret law (including the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act of 2016) for disclosing a Company trade secret in confidence either directly or indirectly to a Federal, State, or local government official, or an attorney, for the sole purpose of reporting, or investigating, a suspected violation of law. Moreover, you may disclose Company trade secrets in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding if such filing is made under seal. Finally, if you decide not to sign this Agreement and thereby release all claims for retaliation, and instead file a lawsuit alleging retaliation by the Company for reporting a suspected violation of the law, you may disclose the Company trade secrets to your attorney(s) and use the trade secrets in the court proceeding, so long as any document containing the trade secrets is filed under seal and you do not otherwise disclose the trade secrets, except pursuant to court order.

VIII. NON-DISPARAGEMENT

You promise not to make any negative or disparaging remarks to any third party, whether verbally or in writing, about the Company or another Releasee or those associated with a Releasee, including but not limited to remarks about the Releasee’s products, services, and business practices, and regardless of whether those remarks are made via any social media forum, networking, blog or other internet site or by some other means.
